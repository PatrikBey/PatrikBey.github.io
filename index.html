Research Focus

Areas of Investigation

AI in Neurology

Applying machine learning and deep learning to neuroimaging data for improved diagnosis and prognosis in dementia, stroke, and other neurological conditions.

  • Dementia classification
  • Stroke recovery prediction
  • Deep graph learning
  • Clinical decision support

Stroke Research

Developing lesion-aware automated processing frameworks for clinical stroke MRI. Investigating group-derived and individual disconnection patterns for recovery prediction.

  • Lesion-aware processing
  • Disconnection analysis
  • Recovery prediction
  • Clinical stroke MRI

Neuroimaging Analysis

Advanced processing and analysis of multi-modal neuroimaging data including fMRI, structural MRI, and PET imaging for clinical and research applications.

  • fMRI signal processing
  • Structural MRI analysis
  • PET imaging integration
  • Multi-modal fusion

Translational Neuroscience

Bridging preclinical and clinical research through graph-based approaches. Our work on serotonin and social behavior in mice demonstrates how network models derived from animal studies can inform human neuroscience.

  • Graph models in animal studies
  • Serotonin-behavior networks
  • Cross-species validation frameworks
  • Preclinical to clinical translation

About

Dr. Patrik Bey

As a Senior Research Fellow at UCL's Queen Square Institute of Neurology, I specialize in the application and analysis of complex AI models to high-dimensional clinical neuroimaging data.

My research focuses on understanding the network organization of the human brain and its response to pathological events such as stroke. I'm particularly interested in bridging the gap between computational neuroscience and clinical applications, developing tools that can directly impact patient care.

Previously, I completed my PhD (Dr. rer. medic.) at Charité Universitätsmedizin Berlin in 2025, with a dissertation on facilitating brain network modeling for pathological patient populations.
